#1 Workshop

Participants:

* Christian Wirth (Uni Leipzig, iDiv)
* Mario Liebergesell (Uni Leipzig, FUN-Div)
* Bettina Ohse (Uni Leipzig, iDiv)
* Claas-Thido Pfaff (Uni Leipzig)

Claas Presented 3 different levels of abstraction for the chemical vocabulary
branch. While the first was formed by names of compounds and a few names of
chemical classes, the second represented a bit more abstract and condensed view
on chemicals based on chemical classes and own categories for classification.
The last one was a highly abstract perspective onto chemical compounds using
features of the compounds for classification (length, branching, ...).

* Discussion

	+ The abstract vocabulary capturing the features of chemical compounds is
	  interesting but may be only partially useful for data annotation and discovery
	  as it rather represents a identification key for chemical compounds. For
	  example who does always know which length a compound has or exact structure?

* Agreements

	+ The abstract stuff might be to abstract as e.g. nobody will know the length of
	  a complex carbohydrate by heart. However the general classification groups like
	  e.g. the separation into Monomer/Polymer is still useful.

	+ We agreed on the need of the category of Biochemical compounds and were
	  browsing literature to find the most relevant concepts and sub-concepts.

	+ We agreed on the importance of a stoichiometry part in the chem. vocabulary
	  This captures the main components of compounds that are described. We have this
	  in place with with the elemental part of the vocabulary which is filled by the
	  periodic system of elements.

	+ We agreed on that it is good to orient the vocabulary after the large groups
	  found in the literature of chemicals


#2 Workshop

Participants:

* Nicol Van Dam (iDiv)
* Katharina Grosser (iDiv)
* Alexander Weinhold (iDiv)
* Mario Liebergesell (Uni Leipzig, FUN-Div)
* Claas-Thido Pfaff (Uni Leipzig)

The results from the first workshop were presented and discussed. The structure
and the contents were found to be good but the level of detail was too high and
thus confusing. Additionally the vocabulary often intermixes the classes with
names of single substances. The participants were going through all the classes
discussing and improving their contents.


* Notes
	+ The classification of lipids into storage and membrane lipids is a functional
	  one. The fact of being a storage or membrane lipid is thus rather a role of the
	  chemical compound than a classification (Removed the classification?)

	+ Something is missing to denote the method the chemicals have been classified. This
	  is something that could go into the methodological branch. However this is rather
	  metadata isn't it?

		Total phenolics (Farbtabelle)
		Total protein (Bradford Method)
		Cyanin (Blaufärbung, Lackmus)


* Agreements

	+ The level of detail is to high for a manual data annotation and data
	  discovery. Thus many items of the classes have been removed from the
	  vocabulary.

	+ If ions are mentioned they should be spelled out with their common name
	  e.g. nitrate, carbonate and not abbreviated no3-
